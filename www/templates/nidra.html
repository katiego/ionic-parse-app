Yoga nidra, also known as yogic sleep, is a powerful guided meditation technique that can be practiced by anyone, at any time. Yoga Nidra focus on cultivating multiple levels of well-being.


Yoga Nidra is...
  • A guided mediation systematically takes you through the panacha maya kosha (five layers of self) to leave you with a deep sense of wholeness.

  • Promotes deep relaxation that isnt found in the average meditation practice. The stages of body scan and breath awareness calm the nervous system, leading to less stress and better health.

  • Offers a space to explore what you need in the moment, as well as release long-held emotions.

How to practice
  • Lay down in a comfortable position, preferably on your back. Be sure to cushion your backside. If you are not able to lay down, you can practice in a seated position.

  • Follow the voice that is guiding you. If you lose the voice, no worries, just start listning again once you realize you've drifted off. Falling asleep is also ok; you will still receive benefits while the unconscious mind is absorbing the practice.

Tips for a sustained, regular practice
  • Meditation is most beneficial when practiced daily, no matter the length. For example, is better for your brain to meditate 5 minutes everyday, instead of 20 minutes 2 times a week.

  • A yoga nidra practice can be as short as five minutes and as long as an hour. Chose the length that fits your schedule and needs.
